Frequently Asked Questions

Salvation is entirely by God's grace through faith alone in Christ alone - not by works, rituals, or moral effort (Ephesians 2:8-9). As John MacArthur emphasizes, saving faith is not mere intellectual assent but a wholehearted surrender to Christ as Lord. True salvation involves repentance - a turning from sin - and faith - a turning to Christ in trust.

When God regenerates your heart, you are justified before God, adopted into His family, and sealed by the Holy Spirit. This is not something you earn - it is a gift received. Calvin wrote: "We are saved by grace alone, but the grace that saves is never alone - it always produces a transformed life."

"If you confess with your mouth the Lord Jesus and believe in your heart that God has raised Him from the dead, you will be saved." - Romans 10:9

God's sovereignty means He exercises absolute authority over all creation - from galaxies to the smallest details of your life. As Calvin wrote in the Institutes: "God so governs all events by His providence that nothing happens by chance."

This is not cold determinism but loving, purposeful rule. As John Piper says: "God is always doing 10,000 things in your life, and you may be aware of three of them." God's sovereignty does not negate human responsibility, but it gives believers profound comfort (Romans 8:28).

"He does according to His will in the army of heaven and among the inhabitants of the earth. No one can restrain His hand." - Daniel 4:35

No. Those whom God has truly saved, He will keep to the end. This is the doctrine of the Perseverance of the Saints. Jesus said: "My sheep hear My voice, and I know them, and they follow Me. And I give them eternal life, and they shall never perish" (John 10:27-28).

MacArthur teaches that genuine salvation always produces evidence - it is not a prayer without transformation. Calvin wrote that the elect are preserved not by their own power but by God's unbreakable covenant. God who began a good work in you will complete it (Philippians 1:6).

The chief end of man is to glorify God and enjoy Him forever - the opening of the Westminster Catechism, deeply aligned with Calvin's theology. John Piper frames this as "Christian Hedonism": God is most glorified in us when we are most satisfied in Him.

Your purpose is not found in career, relationships, or achievements - it is found in knowing, loving, and worshiping God through Christ. "Whatever you do, do all to the glory of God" (1 Corinthians 10:31).

Jesus had to die because God is both perfectly just and perfectly loving. Sin must be punished - God cannot simply overlook it without compromising His justice. Yet God also loves sinners and desires their salvation. The cross is where justice and mercy meet.

This is penal substitutionary atonement - the view held by MacArthur, Piper, and Calvin. Jesus bore the wrath of God against sin in the place of His people. "For He made Him who knew no sin to be sin for us, that we might become the righteousness of God in Him" (2 Corinthians 5:21).

Predestination is God's eternal, sovereign choice to save a specific people for Himself - not based on their foreseen merit, but entirely on His grace. "He chose us in Him before the foundation of the world" (Ephesians 1:4).

As Calvin taught, election is unconditional. This does not make God unfair - as Paul asks in Romans 9, "Who are you to reply against God?" No one deserves salvation, so God's choice to save anyone is pure mercy. Predestination is not a barrier to evangelism but its foundation (2 Thessalonians 2:13-14).

The Bible is self-attesting - its authority does not depend on human approval. As MacArthur teaches, Scripture's inerrancy and divine origin are confirmed by its unity (66 books, 40+ authors, 1500 years, one message), prophetic accuracy, historical reliability, and transforming power.

Calvin called Scripture the "spectacles" through which we see God clearly. Jesus Himself affirmed the Bible's authority: "Scripture cannot be broken" (John 10:35). "All Scripture is given by inspiration of God" (2 Timothy 3:16) - literally "God-breathed."

Being born again (regeneration) is the supernatural work of the Holy Spirit in which He gives a dead sinner new spiritual life. As MacArthur teaches, this is not something you do - it is something done to you.

Jesus told Nicodemus: "Unless one is born of water and the Spirit, he cannot enter the kingdom of God" (John 3:5). Calvin described regeneration as the Spirit renewing the heart so that a person can truly see, desire, and embrace Christ. The evidence is a transformed life: new desires, new affections, new obedience (2 Corinthians 5:17).

The Holy Spirit is God - the third Person of the Trinity - not an impersonal force. He convicts the world of sin (John 16:8); regenerates dead hearts (Titus 3:5); indwells every believer as a seal (Ephesians 1:13-14); sanctifies believers producing fruit (Galatians 5:22-23); and empowers for service.

Piper emphasizes that the Spirit's supreme ministry is to glorify Christ. MacArthur teaches that the Spirit equips believers with gifts for building up the body. "He will glorify Me, for He will take of what is Mine and declare it to you" (John 16:14).

Read the Bible prayerfully, systematically, and with a desire to know God - not merely to gather information. MacArthur advocates for expository reading: letting the text speak for itself, in context, verse by verse.

Start with the Gospels (John is ideal), then Acts, Romans, and the rest of the New Testament. Calvin counseled reading with humility and dependence on the Holy Spirit for illumination. Piper recommends asking: What does this teach about God? About me? What should I do? "Your word is a lamp to my feet and a light to my path" (Psalm 119:105).

The Heart of Faith

Who Is Jesus?

Jesus Christ is the central figure of Christianity - fully God and fully human, the Son of God who came to save the world.

Son of God

Jesus is the only begotten Son of God, born of the Virgin Mary. He is the Word made flesh - God who became human to dwell among us.

"In the beginning was the Word, and the Word was with God, and the Word was God." - John 1:1

Savior of the World

Jesus died on the cross to pay the penalty for our sins. His sacrifice offers forgiveness, redemption, and eternal life to all who believe.

"For God so loved the world that He gave His only begotten Son." - John 3:16

Risen Lord

On the third day, Jesus rose from the dead, conquering death and sin. His resurrection is the foundation of Christian hope and the promise of new life.

"He is not here; for He is risen, as He said." - Matthew 28:6

Foundational Truths

The Five Solas

The five foundational pillars of the Christian faith, recovered during the Reformation and held by faithful Bible teachers throughout history.

I

Sola Scriptura

Scripture Alone

The Bible is the ultimate, infallible authority for faith and practice. As Calvin wrote, Scripture is the "spectacles" through which we see God clearly. No council, tradition, or human teaching can supersede God's Word.

2 Timothy 3:16-17

II

Sola Fide

Faith Alone

Justification before God is received by faith alone, not by works. Calvin taught that faith is the instrument by which we receive Christ and all His benefits. Good works are the fruit - never the root - of salvation.

Romans 3:28

III

Sola Gratia

Grace Alone

Salvation is entirely by God's grace - His unmerited favor toward undeserving sinners. As MacArthur emphasizes, grace is not just God's attitude but His active power to save. No human effort can contribute to salvation.

Ephesians 2:8-9

IV

Solus Christus

Christ Alone

Jesus Christ is the only mediator between God and man. His life, death, and resurrection are completely sufficient for salvation. As Piper says: "Christ did not make salvation possible - He actually saved." No saint, priest, or ritual can add to His finished work.

1 Timothy 2:5

V

Soli Deo Gloria

Glory to God Alone

All of life exists to glorify God. As the Westminster Catechism opens: the chief end of man is to glorify God and enjoy Him forever. Calvin wrote that every aspect of life should be directed toward God's glory.

1 Corinthians 10:31

Ready to Accept Jesus?

If you would like to invite Jesus into your heart, pray this prayer from wherever you are:

"Dear God, I know that I am a sinner and I need Your forgiveness. I believe that Jesus Christ died on the cross for my sins and rose from the dead. I turn from my sins and I invite Jesus to come into my heart and life as my Lord and Savior. I want to follow You and live for You. Thank You for Your love, Your grace, and the gift of eternal life. In Jesus name I pray, Amen."

If you just prayed this prayer, welcome to the family of God! Find a local church, read the Bible, and talk to God daily through prayer.
